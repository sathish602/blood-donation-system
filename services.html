Common Questions

Everything you need to know about the donation process.

You can donate blood every 3 months (about 12 weeks) if you are healthy and meet the eligibility requirements. Regular blood donation helps maintain a safe blood supply and can save lives. Always follow the guidelines of your local blood bank before donating.

Before your blood donation appointment, make sure you are well prepared. Eat a healthy meal, drink plenty of water, get enough sleep, and avoid alcohol or smoking before donating. Wear comfortable clothes with sleeves that can be rolled up easily. Carry a valid ID and follow any instructions given by the blood donation center.

A blood donation usually takes about 30–45 minutes in total. The actual blood collection process takes around 8–10 minutes, while the rest of the time is used for registration, health checks, and a short recovery period after donation.

Yes, donating blood is generally safe for eligible donors. The process uses sterile, single-use equipment to prevent infection. Some people may feel a little tired, dizzy, or experience a small bruise afterward, but these effects are usually temporary. Drinking water and resting after donation can help you feel better.
